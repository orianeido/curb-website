The Complete Hungary Parking Guide: Rules, Signs & Fines

July 1, 2026 · 13 min read · By Curb Editorial Team
images/blog/parking-guide-hungary-hero.webp

Parking in Hungary requires careful attention to yellow line markings, time-based restrictions, and zone designations. From understanding solid, broken, and zig-zag yellow lines to navigating ZONA signs and special parking areas, Hungarian parking rules demand precision. Violating these rules can result in substantial fines and vehicle towing.

This guide decodes every parking sign and restriction you'll encounter on Hungarian roads. Whether you're a resident, visitor, or regular commuter, you'll learn what each sign means, where you can park, and how to avoid costly penalties across Hungary's cities and regions.

Key Takeaways

  • Solid yellow lines mean no stopping and no parking under any circumstances
  • Broken yellow lines allow passenger drop-off/pick-up but prohibit parking
  • Zig-zag yellow lines are the strictest—no stopping or parking
  • Time-based restrictions (periods of time, hours) regulate when parking is allowed
  • ZONA signs mark parking zones; always check supplementary signs for rules
  • Parking fines in Hungary range from 10,000-30,000 HUF (fines)

Understanding Curb Markings: Yellow Lines

Hungarian curb markings are essential for understanding parking rules. Yellow lines are the primary indicators of parking restrictions, with different line styles conveying different levels of restriction. Solid, broken, and zig-zag yellow lines each have distinct meanings that drivers must understand to avoid violations.

Solid Yellow Line (No Stopping, No Parking)

Prohibits both stopping and parking. No vehicles may stop or park along this curb, even briefly.

Common locations:
  • Main roads and traffic-heavy areas
  • Near fire hydrants
  • Bus stops and transit zones
  • Emergency vehicle routes

Fine: 10,000-20,000 HUF (fines)

Broken Yellow Line (Passenger Drop-off/Pick-up Allowed)

No parking allowed, but stopping briefly for dropping off or picking up passengers is permitted.

Rules:
  • Only for passenger transfer
  • Brief stops only (not for loading cargo)
  • Driver must remain in vehicle or nearby
  • Parking is strictly prohibited

Fine: 10,000 HUF (€25) if parking instead of drop-off

Yellow Line Variations & Special Markings

Beyond solid and broken lines, Hungarian parking regulations include zig-zag yellow lines as the most restrictive curb marking. Additionally, time-based and distance-based restrictions provide supplementary information about when and where parking rules apply.

Zig-Zag Yellow Line (Most Restrictive)

Absolute prohibition on both stopping and parking. The strictest yellow line marking in Hungary.

Applied to:
  • School zones during drop-off/pick-up hours
  • Hospital entrances
  • Emergency access routes
  • High-congestion areas

Fine: 15,000-20,000 HUF (fines)

Period of Time (Time Window Restriction)

Parking restricted during specific hours shown on sign (e.g., 06:00-09:30, 15:30-19:00).

How to interpret:
  • Parking prohibited during marked hours
  • Multiple time windows may apply
  • Common in residential areas during peak hours
  • Check sign for exact time ranges

Fine: 10,000-15,000 HUF (fines) for violating time windows

Days of Week & Period of Time

Restrictions apply only on specific days of the week during designated hours.

Example interpretations:
  • Monday-Friday 06:00-20:30: Weekday restrictions
  • Sundays/holidays may have different rules
  • Street cleaning days often have restrictions
  • Always note both day and time requirements

Fine: 10,000-15,000 HUF (fines)

Period of Time in Hours (2 óra)

Maximum parking duration in hours. Sign indicates how long you can stay parked.

Duration examples:
  • 2 óra = 2 hours maximum
  • 1 óra = 1 hour maximum
  • 30 perc = 30 minutes maximum
  • Overstaying results in fines

Fine: 10,000 HUF (€25) for exceeding duration

Length While Sign Is Enforced (Distance Marker)

Indicates how far the parking restriction extends (e.g., ↑ 0,8km ↑).

Understanding distance markers:
  • Shows affected road segment length
  • Restriction applies for entire distance
  • Essential for long streets with extended zones
  • Helps drivers find unrestricted parking beyond the zone

Info: Helps identify exact restriction boundaries

Parking on Sidewalk in Full Formation

Permits parking on the sidewalk with vehicle fully positioned (all wheels on sidewalk).

Rules:
  • Vehicle must be entirely on sidewalk
  • All four wheels positioned correctly
  • Must not obstruct pedestrian pathways
  • Check local regulations for width requirements

Info: Allowed only where sign permits

Parking on Sidewalk with Half Wheel

Permits parking with one set of wheels on sidewalk and one on street (half-on sidewalk).

Application:
  • Vehicle partially on street, partially on sidewalk
  • Specific parking angle indicated on sign
  • Commonly used on narrower streets
  • Must not block pedestrian traffic

Info: Allows more vehicles per street segment

No Parking & Stopping Zones

Hungarian no-parking zones use specific signs to indicate absolute prohibitions. Red circles with blue X symbols denote areas where parking is entirely prohibited. ZONA signs also mark special restricted areas. Understanding these signs is critical to avoiding heavy fines and vehicle towing.

No Parking Zone

Absolute prohibition on parking. Red circle with blue X indicates vehicles will be towed.

Common locations:
  • Near pedestrian crossings
  • Bus stops and taxi stands
  • Fire hydrants and emergency exits
  • Loading zones

Fine: 15,000-25,000 HUF (fines) + possible towing

No Stopping Zone

Prohibition on stopping and parking. No temporary stops allowed, not even for passenger drop-off.

Applies to:
  • All vehicles equally
  • Any duration of stopping
  • No loading, no passenger drop-off
  • Strict enforcement with high fines

Fine: 15,000-25,000 HUF (fines) + possible towing

No Parking Zone (ZONA)

ZONA sign with prohibition symbol indicates a designated no-parking area.

Zone characteristics:
  • Clearly marked zone boundaries
  • Parking prohibited throughout entire zone
  • May have time-based exceptions
  • Check for supplementary signs

Fine: 15,000-25,000 HUF (fines)

End of No Parking Zone

Marks the end of a no-parking restriction. Parking may be allowed beyond this point.

Critical: After this sign, check for new restrictions that may begin immediately. Always look for the next sign before parking.

Authorized Parking Zones & Special Areas

Hungarian parking zones use P signs to mark authorized parking areas. These zones may be free or metered, and some are reserved for specific vehicle types or users. ZONA signs combined with P symbols indicate where parking is permitted, often with restrictions on duration, payment, or vehicle type.

Parking Zone (P ZONA)

Designated parking area where parking is allowed. Check supplementary signs for time limits and payment requirements.

Zone details:
  • May be free or metered parking
  • Time limits may apply
  • Check posted signs for restrictions
  • Boundaries marked by zone signs

Fine: 10,000 HUF (€25) for overstaying or non-payment

End of Parking Zone (ZONA)

ZONA sign with line through it indicates the end of a parking zone restriction.

Important: Previous zone restrictions no longer apply beyond this marker. Check for new signs indicating different rules.

Parking for Trucks (P ZONA)

Reserved exclusively for truck and commercial vehicle parking. General passenger vehicles not permitted.

Rules:
  • For commercial vehicles only
  • Passenger cars prohibited
  • Check weight and size restrictions
  • May have time limitations

Fine: 15,000 HUF (€37) for unauthorized parking

Parking for People with Reduced Mobility

Reserved exclusively for disabled drivers with valid wheelchair or reduced mobility permits.

Requirements:
  • Display valid disabled permit on dashboard
  • Usually free and unlimited parking
  • EU-recognized permits accepted
  • Unauthorized parking prohibited

Fine: 20,000 HUF (€50) for unauthorized parking

Parking Methods & Management Systems

Hungarian parking includes different payment and time-management systems. Parking meters require cash payment or app payment, while parking disc systems allow free parking for a limited time. Some areas use both systems. Understanding which system applies in each zone is essential for avoiding violations.

Parking Meter

Indicates paid parking with parking meter or app payment required.

How to park:
  • Pay at nearby parking meter
  • Display ticket or payment confirmation
  • Check posted rates and time limits
  • Many areas accept app payments

Fine: 10,000 HUF (€25) for non-payment or overstay

Place Clock in Dashboard (Disc Parking)

Indicates disc parking zone. Display parking disc showing arrival time.

Disc parking rules:
  • Free parking with valid disc
  • Set disc to your arrival time
  • Discs available at petrol stations
  • Check sign for maximum duration

Fine: 10,000 HUF (€25) for exceeding time limit or missing disc
